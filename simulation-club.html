The Case for Simulation

Too Expensive

Preferred when real-world alternatives are prohibitively costly, such as building a physical bridge solely to test its ultimate load capacity.

Too Dangerous

Crucial when reality poses physical risks. We can study viral propagation or hazardous reactions in a controlled, risk-free environment.

Impossible

Required when real-time observation is unfeasible. Simulation allows us to project the multi-decade effects of climate change in seconds.

The Model

The system of equations used to capture and define the behavior of a system—the theoretical blueprint.

The Simulation

The execution of algorithms that solve those equations, often employing approximate methods to navigate complexity.

Simulation is the process of running a model. We do not "build a simulation"; we build a model (or simulator) and then run a simulation to observe its behavior.
