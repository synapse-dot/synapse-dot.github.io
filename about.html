Profile

Who I Am

Hi, I’m Synapse. Nice to meet you. I tend to follow random curiosity spirals — the kind that start from small observations and somehow end up turning into whole ideas. A lot of my thinking comes from imagination and noticing odd patterns in everyday things, and I usually don’t stick to one direction for too long.

When I build things, it’s usually experimental at first, but I don’t really stop until I’ve shaped it into at least a rough prototype. I’m more of a “think first, act later (eventually)” kind of person, which leads to a mix of unfinished ideas and sudden bursts of making things real. Somewhere in that process, I ended up as a founder of the Simulation & Modelling Society (MCSMS) — still slightly funny to me, but it stuck.

Interests

What I'm Into

First principles

I like seeing how maths and science are built from the ground up, where structure reveals itself naturally and intuitively. Half time I spiral through confusion, then suddenly it clicks like it was obvious all along.

Computer simulations

Building and experimenting with systems to see how ideas behave in practice. Code lives, moves, and surprises.

Linux

Open systems. Total control. Understanding how things work under the hood. Breaking things to learn how to fix them.

Maths and physics

Even when I don’t fully understand them yet. The struggle is the point. The confusion is just the universe playing hide and seek.

Social satire

Shows like Family Guy. Humour reflects real-world absurdity in unexpected ways. Laughing at the chaos helps.

Leadership

MCSMS Org

As the founder of the Simulation & Modelling Society, I have designed a modular engineering environment that supports over 40 active members. My responsibilities include:

  • Designing standardized project templates and documentation frameworks.
  • Automating workflows using GitHub Actions and custom tooling.
  • Mentoring students in computational physics and systems design.
