Profile

Who I Am

Hi, I’m Synapse. Nice to meet you. I tend to follow random curiosity spirals — the kind that start from small observations and somehow end up turning into whole ideas. A lot of my thinking comes from imagination and noticing odd patterns in everyday things, and I usually don’t stick to one direction for too long.

When I build things, it’s usually experimental at first, but I don’t really stop until I’ve shaped it into at least a rough prototype. I’m more of a “think first, act later (eventually)” kind of person, which leads to a mix of unfinished ideas and sudden bursts of making things real. Somewhere in that process, I ended up as a founder of the Simulation & Modelling Society (MCSMS) — still slightly funny to me, but it stuck.

Interests

What I'm Into

First principles

I like seeing how maths and science are built from the ground up, where the structure reveals itself naturally and intuitively. Half the time I spiral through confusion, and then suddenly it clicks like it was obvious all along.

Computer simulations

I build systems just to watch what happens when they run. There’s something satisfying about setting things up and feeling like I accidentally created a tiny universe.

Linux

I like systems that don’t hide things from you. Control, customization, and integration are the main appeal… plus the quiet joy of knowing you can probably fix anything (eventually).

Maths and physics

I usually spiral a bit, question my life choices, and then occasionally reach a moment where everything makes sense for no apparent reason.

Dark humour

Especially absurd humour like Family Guy, where reality is exaggerated just enough that you’re not sure whether to laugh or question humanity a little.
